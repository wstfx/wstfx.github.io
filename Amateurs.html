Amateurs

Art & Design

I enjoy visual problem-solving. I’m comfortable in Photoshop and Premiere Pro for posters, covers, and short edits, and I’ve begun experimenting with After Effects for motion. I want to study drawing systematically to deepen composition, color, and form—so my design work feels intentional, not just polished.

Economics & Society

Beyond engineering, I follow economics and finance as a way to understand how systems behave. I’m curious about how policy, incentives, and institutions shape everyday outcomes, and I often connect these readings back to technology and operations.

Reading

I read contemporary fiction and serious literature across countries. Recent: Everything I Never Told You. Long-time favorites include Midnight’s Children, Dance, Dance, Dance, The Discomfort of Evening, Dance of the Happy Shades, and Der Fuchs war damals schon der Jäger. Most of these I first read in high school and keep revisiting for their voices and structures.

Music

Mostly English pop and country pop, with occasional detours into other genres and languages when something catches my ear. Music scores my study sessions and bike rides, and sometimes drives the pacing of video edits.

Aesthetic Preferences

My “soul animal” is the fox. I gravitate to green, yellow, and orange—colors that feel alive and optimistic. I like clean lines, high contrast, and a bit of playfulness.

Everyday Joys

I collect plush toys. I like going out with friends. I ride bikes for the fresh air and the thinking time. One day I’d love to keep a cat or a dog (maybe both).
