About Me

Third-year undergraduate from Westlake University (China), exchange student at Cornell University for Fall 2025. I work across AI/ML and human-centered tech—ranging from diffusion models for materials to VR teaching tools—and I led a first-author qualitative study on the well-being of Deaf/Hard-of-Hearing students. I'm fluent in Python/PyTorch, C++, Verilog, and comfortable moving from code to design and communication with various tools. What teammates usually notice first is my execution speed and willingness to own the problem.

I like building things that matter the next day: models that inform decisions, interfaces that improve understanding, and processes that make operations smoother. This is why my interests span technology, operations, investing, and product work—the through-line is turning good ideas into reliable value.

Quick snapshot: B.S. in Electronic Information Engineering at Westlake (GPA 4.04/4.3; TOEFL 107), Fall 2025 at Cornell ECE/ORIE/InfoSci. Former president of the Westlake College Student Union. Research assistant in AI-for-Science at Westlake and the Virtual Embodiment Lab at Cornell.

Thoughts and Experiences

Educational Experience

I grew up in China on a traditional school track but spent after-school time exploring “interest classes” — drawing, calligraphy, creative thinking, and sports — rather than just test prep. In middle school, a kind science teacher opened her office to my questions; physics and chemistry clicked, math followed, and I learned to study from first principles rather than memorize.

High school brought competition and noise. Physics Olympiad training reframed science for me as a coherent system, not a bag of formulas. I later returned to the standard track with about 100 days to prep for the Gaokao, built my own plan, and finished around the top 0.37% in my province. When it came time to choose a university, I picked Westlake for its research-driven culture and room to build. That choice led to deep lab work, student leadership, and eventually an exchange at Cornell.

At Westlake I learned the backbone of research — reproducible environments, Git workflows, careful data work — and explored AI-for-materials, ASR, and active learning. At Cornell I'm expanding into operations and HCI while helping design a VR experience to teach special relativity. Across both campuses, the lesson has been consistent: depth beats points, and the right mentors change your trajectory.

Professional Experiences

I haven't held a formal industry role yet; I'm actively seeking internships or full-time opportunities where I can ship useful work and learn modern execution at scale.

Specifically, these are the fields I want to work in:

Investing: study markets, incentives, and unit economics to judge whether technology creates durable value beyond the demo.
Consulting: step into live organizations to map constraints, remove bottlenecks, and deliver measurable operational gains.
Tech / Product: build user-facing systems—translate needs into architecture and interfaces, ship thoughtfully, and iterate until outcomes improve.
Data analysis: turn models into decisions with clean pipelines, trustworthy metrics, and causal tests that show what actually moved the needle.

I'm strongest where math, physics, coding, and modeling meet operations and product sense. My strategy isn't to narrow into a single silo but to build a coherent toolkit — technical depth, empirical taste, and an understanding of how organizations create value — so that when fields intersect, I can raise the ceiling rather than just follow the playbook.
